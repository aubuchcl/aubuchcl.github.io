Heading to DBC

First Impressions
First impressions of something are a sensitive subject. I don't really know how I feel about the DBC experience or what to expect. I am feeling a melancholy that I think is inspired by the amount of anxiety and excitement being nearly equal. I think the experience will be life changing. I can say that with confidence. When I moved to the west coast from the midwest to become a ski instructor, I was in a similar boat. I had never taught a thing in my life and I was working with a two week program that was an intensive style similar to DBC. I was feeling somewhat similar to how I feel now, but I at that point knew one thing: This experience will change my life. On an objective basis, I do like the layout of the program. I think it is aggressive enough to keep a high valence of performance and structured enough to deliver on its goals. The curriculum seems relevant and well thought out. I am excited to live in San Fran because, I believe, it is the epicenter of technology right now.



What are my impressions of DBC
Right now I am thinking of DBC as an opportunity. Its like an empty box that I can fill with experiences and opportunity. So I guess I don't really have a impresssion of DBC right now.


How will I engage
I see myself engaging with DBC the same way as I engaged with roller coasters when I was a kid. It was scary, but seemed fun and I didn't want to miss it. So you strap in and hold on and when you get scared you try not to think about it. When I am done I am sure that I will be super excited and ready to move onto the next coaster. Each time I complete a coaster I was less scared and more prepared for the next and thats how I feel each week/phase will go. I don't know what else I could say about how I will respond or engage. I guess at least its not highlander.
